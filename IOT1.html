Introduction - Internet-of-Things (IoT)

The term IoT, or Internet of Things, refers to the collective network of connected devices and the technology that facilitates communication between devices and the cloud, as well as between the devices themselves. Thanks to the advent of inexpensive computer chips and high bandwidth telecommunication, we now have billions of devices  connected to the internet. This means everyday devices like toothbrushes, vacuums, cars, and machines can use sensors to collect data and respond intelligently to users.  

"... is the concept of connecting any device(...) to the Internet and to other connected devices. The IoT is a giant network of connected things and people - all of which collect and share data about the way they are used and about the environment around them. " IBM

1.      What is the difference between physical computing and IoT? Please select two right answers.

a)      The physical computing concept involves interacting with hardware components and IoT concepts to make those components accessible over a network. 
b)     Physical computing is an underpinning technology to drive IoT applications. 
c)      IoT is better than physical computing devices. 
d)     A physical computing device is an independent device that cannot connect to other devices whereas IoT devices can.   

2.      What is the correct statement for the physical computing concept?

a)      A physical hardware component that can sense and respond to the world around it. 
b)     An intangible software installed on the embedded microcontroller-based system that can sense, reason and transmit/receive data to another device. 
c)      None of the above 
d)     A virtual interactive system that can simulate the world around it and/or control the outputs, such as lights, displays and motors.   

3.       What can we do with a physical computing device?

a)      All of these. 
b)     Transmit and receive data to another device. 
c)      Sense, reason and react. 
d)     Embed microcontrollers within Things!

1. a) and b)

Explanation: Physical computing involves interacting with hardware components to sense and respond to the world around it. IoT (Internet of Things) extends this concept by connecting these physical computing devices to a network, enabling them to communicate and share data.

2. a)

Explanation: This is the most accurate definition of a physical computing system. It emphasizes the tangible aspect of hardware interacting with the environment through sensors and actuators.

3. a)

Explanation: All the given options are correct.

Physical computing interacts with hardware, while IoT builds upon it to connect devices remotely.


Question. Have you used/experienced IoT devices in your daily life? How and what kind of projects did you build? If you have created IoT projects previously, - what was the most challenging project and how did you overcome the challenges? - what were the key features? Do not worry if you have limited experience. Type IoT projects on Google or YouTube and you will find plenty of DIY and commercial projects to inspire you.

IoT projects can vary widely depending on the application and purpose. Some common types of IoT projects include home automation, industrial monitoring, health monitoring, smart agriculture, and more. Here are a few examples of IoT projects and their key features:

1. Home Automation System:
Key Features:
- Remote control of home appliances (lights, thermostat, door locks, etc.).
- Integration with voice assistants like Amazon Alexa or Google Assistant.
- Security features such as smart cameras and door/window sensors.


2. Smart Agriculture System:
Key Features:
Soil moisture monitoring to optimize irrigation.
Temperature and humidity sensors for climate control.
Automated pest control systems.
Crop health monitoring using drones or sensors.


3. Industrial Monitoring System:
Key Features:
- Predictive maintenance using sensors to monitor machine health.
- Real-time tracking of inventory and assets.
- Environmental monitoring for safety and compliance.


4. Health Monitoring Wearables:
Key Features:
- Continuous monitoring of vital signs (heart rate, temperature, etc.).
- Integration with mobile apps for health data analysis.
- Emergency alerts in case of abnormal health parameters.

The most challenging aspect of an IoT project can depend on various factors, such as the complexity of the system, integration of different sensors and actuators, data security, and scalability. Overcoming challenges often involves a combination of technical skills, problem-solving, and sometimes collaboration with others who have complementary expertise.
